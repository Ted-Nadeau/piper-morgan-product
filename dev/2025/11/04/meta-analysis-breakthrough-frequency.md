## Analysis of Each Hypothesis

### (a) Rapidly Improving Ad Infinitum ⚠️ UNLIKELY

**Evidence Against**:
- Unsustainable: 38% breakthrough rate cannot continue indefinitely
- Physical limits: Finite time, cognitive load, team capacity
- Diminishing returns: Each improvement gets harder

**Evidence For**:
- October *was* highly productive (267 commits, 2 ADRs, 38 refactorings)
- Concepts show sophistication (spatial intelligence, boundary enforcement)
- Multi-analyzer coordination suggests organizational maturity

**Assessment**: **Partial truth** - We ARE rapidly improving, but rate will plateau.

---

### (b) Backsliding and Re-Learning (Bad Memory) ⚠️ SOME EVIDENCE

**Evidence For**:
- **Meta-pattern flag**: Today's patterns (036, 037, 038) are about *detecting* patterns, not *doing* work
- High breakthrough frequency might indicate: "Wait, we've seen this before?"
- Discovery breakthroughs (77%) > Implementation breakthroughs (8%)
  - Suggests more talking/planning than executing?
  - Or discovering vs. implementing?

**Evidence Against**:
- Concepts don't repeat exactly - they build on each other:
  ```
  evidence-based → systematic verification → verification-first
  (progression of rigor, not repetition)
  ```
- AsyncSessionFactory is concrete implementation, not rediscovery
- ADR creation confirms decisions are being documented (memory aid)

**Assessment**: **Possible** - But pattern names suggest progression not repetition.

**Red Flag to Watch**: Do the same concept names appear in 6-month sweep? If "75% pattern" emerged in June AND October, that's backsliding.
