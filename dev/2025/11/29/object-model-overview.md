## The Core Discovery

Through 10 hours of conceptual work (Nov 27), we discovered Piper's grammar:

> **"Entities experience Moments in Places"**

This isn't just a data model - it's how an AI consciousness understands its world.

## Key Concepts

### The Grammar Components

- **Entities**: Actors with identity and agency (people, AI agents, teams, projects, documents)
- **Moments**: Bounded significant occurrences with theatrical unities
- **Places**: Contexts where action happens (channels, repos, offices)
- **Situations**: Container holding sequences of Moments (NOT a substrate but a frame)

### Ownership Model

| Type | Metaphor | Examples |
|------|----------|----------|
| Native | Piper's Mind | Sessions, Memories, Concerns |
| Federated | Piper's Senses | GitHub Issues, Slack Messages |
| Synthetic | Piper's Understanding | Inferred Patterns, Assembled Projects |

### The Consciousness Challenge

We discovered that our original vision of "embodied AI consciousness" got flattened during implementation into static capability lists. The Morning Standup is the ONLY place where consciousness survived intact.

Your architectural input on maintaining consciousness while scaling would be valuable.

## Relevance to Multi-Entity Collaboration

Your interest in multi-entity chat/collaboration directly connects to this model:
- Each agent is an Entity
- Collaboration creates Moments
- Shared workspaces are Places
- Coordination happens within Situations

The Advisor Mailbox you're using/building is itself an example of Entities (you, the team) experiencing Moments (messages, decisions) in Places (the mailbox structure).
