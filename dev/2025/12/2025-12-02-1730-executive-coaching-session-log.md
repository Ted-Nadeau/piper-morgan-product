## Session Purpose

xian requested executive coaching support for transitioning from hands-on execution to strategic leadership as Piper Morgan grows in complexity, involving both human collaborators and multiple AI agents.

---

## Key Themes Discussed

### 1. The Core Challenge: Context Switching as Cognitive Burden

xian identified that the main challenge isn't delegation per se, nor losing sight of the big picture, but rather **the cognitive burden of context switching itself**. The project has reached a level requiring abstraction and leverage that organic, ad-hoc approaches can no longer sustain.

**Ted Nadeau's insight** (shared by xian): A leader's job at a certain point is "not to do anything at all" - to unblock, decide, and encourage rather than execute. This represents a platonic ideal that xian acknowledges may not be fully achievable on a project that started as a solo effort with AI tools, but recognizes the directional truth.

### 2. Captain vs Pilot Metaphor

xian articulated a key distinction: the goal is to be **captain but not pilot** - maintaining overall command and vision without being at the controls for every maneuver.

**Captain-level responsibilities identified:**
- Vision and ethics stewardship
- Ethos and values of the project
- Reinforcing fundamental decisions to prevent drift and chaos
- Quality and worthiness of the product (North Star)
- Maintaining focus while widening scope

### 3. Trial and Error Discovery of Attention Boundaries

xian described learning dynamically (often by trial and error) where attention is genuinely needed versus where processes can be automated or templatized. Two directions of discovery:

- **Attention needed**: Recognizing "I can't be asleep at the wheel on this" - needing to validate, review, double-check
- **Automation opportunities**: Recognizing "there's no reason I need to keep doing this manually" - candidates for templates, rituals, processes, code

### 4. Constitutional/Governance Design Work

The conversation identified an inflection point: moving from **organic discovery to systematic formalization**. This involves:

- Designing how work gets done (always part of this kind of work)
- Making process decisions into "code and law" - literally automated routines, or documented in ADRs, patterns, roadmaps
- Recognizing this meta-level work as essential but requiring deliberate investment

### 5. Cadences and Rhythms

xian described existing organic cadences that have emerged:

- **Daily**: Mental standup (informal); Piper Morgan standup feature (blocked on login)
- **Sprints**: Content-based rather than time-boxed, with natural beginning/middle/end
- **Weekly**: Document audit; Chief of Staff operational check-in
- **Monthly**: Pattern review across documentation

The challenge: formalizing these into commitments, automating/scheduling them, providing scaffolding for consistent application.

### 6. When to Pause for Constitutional Design

Key question identified: **What signals indicate it's time for meta-level governance work vs continuing operational execution?**

This remains an open question xian is sitting with.

## Key Frameworks Surfaced

### From Research

- **Mission Command**: Tell people what effect to achieve and why; let them decide how. "Commander's intent" enables action without further orders.
- **HRO Five Principles** (Weick & Sutcliffe): Preoccupation with failure, reluctance to simplify, sensitivity to operations, commitment to resilience, deference to expertise
- **Gawande's Checklist Distinction**: Errors of ignorance vs errors of ineptitude - checklists address the latter
- **Process Debt**: Sub-optimal processes that have short-term benefits but medium/long-term costs (estimated 30% revenue loss from inefficient workflows)

### From xian

- **Ballet metaphor**: Going from pas de deux to ballet - choreographing multiple performers with different rhythms while maintaining artistic coherence
- **Constitutional design**: Building governance structures that become organizational law, consulted by agents and humans alike
