### Objects of Attention: The Lazy Instantiation Pattern

From the attached conversation—a key insight from CloudOn mobile work:

**The problem**: Fractal ontology. Is "the Q1 roadmap" an entity? Is "that conversation with Sarah" an entity? Is "the word 'gestures'" an entity? Pre-registering everything leads to overwhelming setup, false positives, or undercoverage.

**CloudOn's solution**: Objects are user-defined through interaction. Something becomes a registered object only after the user touches it and starts interacting.

**Applied to Piper**: Entities crystallize through attention. User keeps coming back to "the Sarah situation" → it graduates from mention to registered entity with history, relationships, lifecycle.

**Applied to PM artifacts**: Maybe PRDs emerge when something accumulates enough attention, not when someone formally decides to write one. The pile of discussions, decisions, and notes becomes a "thing" through gravity, not declaration.

**Connection to consciousness model**: Piper's "dreaming" (Session Journal → Insight Journal composting) is exactly this—noticing what the user keeps attending to and promoting it to entity status.
