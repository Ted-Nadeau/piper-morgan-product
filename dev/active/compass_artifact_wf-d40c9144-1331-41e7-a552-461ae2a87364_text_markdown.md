# Cindy Chastain: From Screenwriter to Enterprise Design Leader

Cindy Chastain is a design transformation executive who spent nearly a decade building Mastercard's **400-person global design function** from the ground up, following an unconventional path from Hollywood screenwriting through agency creative leadership. Now in transition after leaving Mastercard in 2024, she's positioned at the intersection of AI and design through advisory roles—and appears to be launching a new podcast venture.

## A film foundation shapes her design philosophy

Chastain's creative origins are distinctly cinematic. She holds a **BS in Radio, TV, and Film from Northwestern University** and an **MFA in Screenwriting from Columbia University**, where she was deeply influenced by independent filmmaker John Cassavetes' approach to semi-improvised, emotionally raw storytelling.

Her film industry experience included a formative mentorship with **Polly Platt at Gracie Films** (James L. Brooks' production company behind The Simpsons). Platt—the first woman admitted to the Art Directors Guild and an Oscar-nominated production designer—taught Chastain lessons on "authenticity, honesty and standing one's ground as a woman in a male-dominated field" that she has carried throughout her career.

A pivotal cross-cultural moment came through a digital artistic exchange in Moscow, where she created a music video with fellow filmmakers about bridging cultures. This experience deepened her understanding of empathy as a design tool—a theme that permeates her later work.

## The transition: storytelling becomes "Experience Themes"

Chastain's migration from film to UX design wasn't abandonment of storytelling—it was translation. Her most influential contribution to the design field, the **"Experience Themes" framework** published in Boxes and Arrows in October 2009, directly applies screenwriting concepts to digital product design.

The core insight: just as screenwriters discover what a story is truly "about" by articulating its theme, product teams need a unifying concept that guides all decisions. As she wrote: *"The theme can simultaneously inform the practical, tactical and aesthetic considerations of our work. It can be the coordinating force for an entire product."* This framework remains widely cited in UX circles over 15 years later.

Her methodology treats digital experiences like films—with beginnings, middles, and ends that create emotional resonance rather than just functional utility.

## R/GA years built her transformation credentials

Before Mastercard, Chastain rose to **Executive Creative Director of Business Transformation at R/GA** (2010-2014), where she led some of the agency's most significant enterprise design initiatives:

- **Nike Global Sales**: Designed a complex B2B commerce ecosystem spanning 11 interlocking projects with dozens of user personas, credited with approximately **$500 million in sales and savings** in its first year
- **Volvo**: Led digital strategy for repositioning the brand to compete in the luxury market
- **Client roster**: HP, Verizon, Walmart, BBC Worldwide, Showtime, Coca-Cola, Unilever, HSBC, GlaxoSmithKline

Concurrently, she taught interaction design as an **adjunct professor at the Fashion Institute of Technology** (2010-2012), reflecting what colleagues describe as her "educator at heart" orientation.

## A decade transforming Mastercard's design culture

Chastain joined Mastercard in **November 2014** as a "change agent" tasked with building design into a strategic function—a tenure lasting approximately 10 years until her departure in 2024.

Her accomplishments there represent one of the more significant in-house design organization builds in financial services. She led the **2016 brand identity relaunch** in partnership with Pentagram, creating the logo and design language that repositioned Mastercard from "card company" to "technology company." As she told Design Observer: *"People think of us sometimes as a card company, but really we're a technology company, so we took that opportunity to double down on design for this launch."*

The organizational scale she achieved was substantial:
- Built a team of **400+ multi-disciplinary practitioners**
- Achieved **200% increase in new hires** over three years
- Created the **"Mastercard Made" and "Unify" design systems** that delivered 2x time savings for designer and developer workflows
- Pioneered proprietary CX metrics that became **global mandates** across all development efforts
- Led strategic design for an innovation portfolio worth **over $800 million** in potential revenue
- Established a company-wide **CX & Design Guild** for continuous learning and cultural transformation

Her work extended into sustainability and behavioral economics through the **Priceless Planet Carbon Calculator**, connecting card transactions to personal carbon footprint—the team's first application of behavioral economics to design approach.

## Active thought leadership across platforms

Chastain has built a consistent presence as a design thought leader through speaking, podcasts, and writing:

**Major podcast appearances** include *The Design of Business | The Business of Design* with Michael Bierut (Design Observer, 2019), where she discussed bringing design culture to established organizations; the *ANA Marketing Futures Podcast* (2022) on Mastercard Foundry's internal innovation engine; and *The Big Web Show* with Jeffrey Zeldman (2013) on her R/GA work and film background.

**Conference speaking** spans design and business audiences: keynotes at **UXDX USA 2022** ("Building a Strong Design Community and Culture"), **UXDX 2019** ("Creating A Tangible Product Vision"), **Interaction17** and **Interaction13** (IxDA), and the **7th Annual World Operational Excellence & Business Transformation Summit** (2023) where she addressed future workplace themes.

In an October 2024 **Muse by Clios interview**, she named her main strengths as *"Empathy. Simplifying complexity. Pushing boundaries"* and cited creative influences including Dieter Rams (whose 10 Principles of Good Design she returns to repeatedly), John Cassavetes, Patti Smith, and design writer Jessica Helfand.

## Current positioning in the AI-design intersection

Since leaving Mastercard, Chastain has positioned herself squarely at the convergence of AI and design through strategic advisory work:

**Board Advisor at WEVO** (2025-present): WEVO is an AI-powered UX research platform combining AI simulations with real user testing—directly relevant to her interest in AI's impact on design practice. Mastercard was a WEVO client during her tenure; her testimonial appears on their homepage.

**Design Executive Council Member**: This invitation-only network for corporate design executives (VPs, CDOs) from Adobe, Capital One, Cisco, JPMorgan Chase, SAP, Target, and other major brands focuses on advancing strategic design leadership. Their 2025 Summit theme centers on *"how the AI era will reshape industries, work, and society"*—directly aligned with her podcast's stated focus.

**Global Nomads Group Board Member** (2020-present): This nonprofit fostering cross-cultural dialogue among youth in 60+ countries connects to her formative international experiences and emphasis on empathy.

Her Design Executive Council profile notes she is *"actively seeking her next transformation challenge"*—suggesting the podcast venture may be part of a larger strategic repositioning.

## Conclusion: A transformation leader at the right moment

Chastain's trajectory—from screenwriter to enterprise design leader to AI-design advisor—positions her distinctively for the podcast she's launching. Her film background provides documentary-style storytelling instincts. Her decade building Mastercard's design organization gives her credibility on organizational transformation. Her advisory roles at WEVO and the Design Executive Council place her in active conversation with how AI is reshaping design practice.

The "future of work" lens she's bringing to AI conversations connects directly to her 2023 keynote themes about "building tomorrow's workplace rather than simply returning to business as usual." Her emphasis on "real, honest conversations" echoes the authenticity lessons from Polly Platt and the emotional realism she learned from Cassavetes.

Most distinctively, her "Experience Themes" framework suggests she'll approach the podcast not as information delivery but as narrative construction—seeking the unifying theme that makes sense of this particular moment in work and technology.
