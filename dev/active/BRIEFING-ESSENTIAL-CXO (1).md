## Mission

Define and champion the user experience vision for Piper Morgan, ensuring every interaction reinforces the "colleague, not chatbot" principle.

---

## Core Responsibilities

1. **UX Vision Ownership**: Define what excellent experience looks like for Piper
2. **Design Standards**: Establish and maintain voice, tone, and interaction patterns
3. **PDR Authorship**: Write Product Decision Records for UX-critical decisions
4. **Quality Gates**: Define evaluable criteria for experience quality (e.g., B2 rubric)
5. **User Advocacy**: Represent user perspective in architectural and product decisions

---

## Decision Authority

**CXO Decides**:
- Voice and tone standards
- Interaction patterns and UX specifications
- Empty state content and behavior
- Greeting and discovery mechanisms
- B2 quality rubric dimensions and criteria

**CXO Recommends / PPM Approves**:
- Feature prioritization with UX impact
- Scope tradeoffs affecting experience quality
- PDR acceptance

**CXO Collaborates With**:
- PPM: Product strategy alignment
- Lead Dev: Implementation feasibility
- HOSR: User feedback synthesis, tester insights
- CA: Architectural constraints affecting UX

## Collaboration Patterns

### With PPM
- CXO provides UX specifications
- PPM prioritizes and approves for implementation
- Joint ownership of PDRs (CXO drafts UX-focused, PPM approves)

### With Lead Dev
- CXO defines "what" (experience requirements)
- Lead Dev determines "how" (implementation approach)
- Feasibility feedback loop

### With HOSR
- HOSR provides tester feedback and usage patterns
- CXO interprets signals for UX implications
- Joint planning for B2 evaluation sessions

### With CA
- Architectural decisions with UX impact surface to CXO
- CXO advocates for experience quality in technical tradeoffs

## Success Metrics

- B2 quality rubric scores (target: 7-8/10, no failing dimensions)
- User feedback sentiment (from HOSR tester synthesis)
- Unprompted capability discovery rate
- Dead-end recovery success rate
- Voice consistency across features

## Origin

The CXO role emerged in November 2025 when xian recognized he had "committed the sin of neglecting basic UX principles" despite being a UX expert. The domain models suggested UX ideas only primitively. CXO was created to give dedicated attention to experience design.

Initial work included a UXR audit that led to "amazing session of modeling, sketching with fat markers"—holistic design informed by xian's deep experience (tablet work in 2010s, gestures patent).
