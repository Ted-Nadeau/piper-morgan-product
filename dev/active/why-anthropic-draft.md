# Why Anthropic? (Draft v1)

*Target: 200-400 words. Current: ~350 words*

---

For the past eight months, I've been building Piper Morgan—an AI-powered product management assistant—almost entirely through collaboration with Claude. Not just using Claude as a coding assistant, but developing systematic methodology for multi-agent coordination: Claude Opus as "Chief Architect" for strategic decisions, Sonnet as "Lead Developer" for implementation, Claude Code for agentic execution. I've documented this publicly, including the failures, because I believe we're at an inflection point where the craft of human-AI collaboration matters as much as the underlying models.

This work has given me an unusual vantage point. I experience Claude's capabilities and limitations daily—not as a researcher studying benchmarks, but as a practitioner shipping features. I know where the product delights (the way Projects maintain context across sessions) and where it frustrates (the manual choreography required to coordinate multiple agents). I have strong opinions about where consumer AI needs to go, and I'm already prototyping some of those ideas.

But Piper Morgan is a side project. What draws me to Anthropic is the opportunity to bring this practitioner's perspective to products that serve millions.

My career has prepared me for exactly this moment. At 7 Cups, I built a mental health platform where user trust wasn't a feature—it was the product. We reached profitability without VC funding by obsessing over the details that made people feel safe enough to be vulnerable. At CloudOn, I led product through a successful Dropbox acquisition, learning what it means to scale consumer experiences to 9 million users. At California's Office of Data & Innovation, I shipped COVID19.ca.gov to 1.5 million daily visitors during a crisis where getting it right mattered.

What connects these experiences is a belief that the best products emerge from genuine understanding of how people actually use them. I've spent 15 years as a product leader, written books on product management and social interface design, and built communities around the intersection of UX and product craft. Now I want to apply that to the product that I believe will define how humans and AI work together.

Claude is already my most important collaborator. I'd like to help make it everyone's.
