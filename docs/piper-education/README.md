# Piper Education: Development Patterns and Practices

**Purpose**: Comprehensive educational resource for AI-assisted development patterns, frameworks, and methodologies

## Organization Structure

This directory is organized by **type** with **source** subcategories to clearly separate established practices from emergent discoveries.

### **Type-Based Organization**

- **Frameworks**: Architectural and development frameworks
- **Decision Patterns**: Patterns for making effective decisions
- **Methodologies**: Development and product management methodologies

### **Source Subcategories**

- **Established**: Received wisdom, best practices, and established methodologies
- **Emergent**: Patterns and practices discovered through our development process

## Key Distinctions

### **Established vs. Emergent**

**Established Practices**:

- Received wisdom and best practices
- Consciously adopted and applied
- Include citations and references to original sources
- Examples: Domain-Driven Design, Test-First Development, ADR Pattern

**Emergent Practices**:

- Discovered through our development process
- Emerged organically from problem-solving
- Include evolution history and problem-solution mapping
- Examples: Session Log Pattern, Human-AI Collaboration Referee, CQRS-Lite

### **Type Categories**

**Frameworks**: Architectural patterns and development frameworks that provide structure and guidance for system design and implementation.

**Decision Patterns**: Patterns for making effective decisions in development, architecture, and product management.

**Methodologies**: Systematic approaches to development, project management, and team collaboration.

## Usage Guidelines

1. **Start with Implementation Guides**: For practical application, begin with the implementation guides
2. **Understand Context**: Read case studies to understand real-world application
3. **Choose by Need**: Select patterns based on specific problems or requirements
4. **Combine Approaches**: Mix established and emergent practices as appropriate
5. **Contribute Back**: Document new emergent practices as they are discovered
