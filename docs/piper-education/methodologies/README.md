# Methodologies

**Purpose**: Systematic approaches to development, project management, and team collaboration

## Organization

### **Established Methodologies** (Established (coming soon))

Received wisdom and best practices for development and project management that we consciously adopted and applied.

**Key Characteristics**:

- Well-documented and widely adopted
- Include citations and references to original sources
- Consciously chosen and implemented
- Examples: Three-Phase Evolution, Session Management, Test-First Development

### **Emergent Methodologies** ([./emergent/](./emergent/))

Methodologies that emerged organically through our development process.

**Key Characteristics**:

- Discovered through problem-solving and iteration
- Include evolution history and problem-solution mapping
- Emerged from real-world challenges
- Examples: Pattern-Driven Development, AI-Assisted Development

#### **Critical Emergent Methodologies**

**Human-AI Collaboration Referee** ([./emergent/human-ai-collaboration-referee.md](./emergent/human-ai-collaboration-referee.md))

- **Pattern Strength**: 15/16 (Critical Strength)
- **Purpose**: Manage effective collaboration between human developers and AI assistants
- **Key Features**: Role definition, handoff protocols, validation gates, conflict resolution
- **Real Examples**: Foundation Sprint Multi-Agent Coordination, PM-012 Parallel Development, PM-021 Error Handling Fix

## Usage Guidelines

1. **Start with Established**: Begin with established methodologies for proven approaches
2. **Enhance with Emergent**: Use emergent methodologies to address specific challenges
3. **Combine Approaches**: Mix established and emergent methodologies as appropriate
4. **Document Processes**: Track methodology effectiveness and outcomes
5. **Share Discoveries**: Contribute emergent methodologies back to the community
