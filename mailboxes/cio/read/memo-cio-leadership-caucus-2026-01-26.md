## Context

During the Jan 16-22 workstream review, HOSR flagged the "Leadership Cascade" from January 19 as a coordination win worth documenting. After discussion, PM renamed it **Leadership Caucus** to better reflect its iterative, facilitated nature (vs. implying a waterfall handoff).

## What Happened (Jan 19)

MUX Track V1 needed to move from vision to implementation. Rather than sequential handoffs, PM facilitated a group discussion:

1. **CXO** contributed design philosophy and consciousness principles
2. **PPM** added product guidance, Time Lord thinking, and suggested Phase 4.5 for canonical query tagging
3. **Chief Architect** provided technical direction and made key decisions (protocols over inheritance)
4. **Lead Dev** coordinated execution with subagents
5. **PM** facilitated, ensured alignment, captured decisions

**Result**: MUX P1 delivered in 15 minutes with 101 tests passing.

## The Question

Is the Leadership Caucus worth formalizing as a coordination pattern?

**Arguments for**:
- Demonstrated clear value (speed through alignment, quality through perspective)
- Distinct from existing patterns (not Audit Cascade, not simple handoff)
- Replicable for future complex, cross-cutting work
- Complements the mailbox system (async) with a synchronous option

**Arguments against**:
- May be situational rather than systematic
- Could add overhead if over-applied
- Might already be implicit in how we work

## Your Input Requested

As CIO (methodology owner), we'd value your perspective:

1. Does this warrant a formal pattern, or is it just "good collaboration"?
2. If worth formalizing, what would the pattern template look like? (Trigger conditions, participants, facilitation protocol, outputs)
3. Any risks of formalizing this that we should consider?

No urgency - this is for ongoing methodology evolution, not a current blocker.
